### 1. The **Money Languages**

Stop thinking about “fun to learn.” Think “employers throw cash at this.”

- **Python** – king of data, AI, automation. It’s the Swiss army knife of big companies and startups.
- **Java** – boring but unstoppable. Core of enterprise, banks, telecom, and it’s an HR filter for a lot of US jobs.
- **Go** – the new blood for cloud, Kubernetes, networking, microservices. A Go engineer with cloud chops is golden.
- **Rust** – high-performance, safety, systems-level credibility. Rust gets you hired by the people building engines, not just using them.
- **Typescript** – full-stack and SaaS world. Shows you can handle the web side of a product when needed.

### 2. **Systems Knowledge That Separates Men From Script Kiddies**

Silicon Valley recruiters don’t care if you made a CRUD app. They care if you understand **systems**.

- **Operating systems**: processes, threads, memory, networking. Real Linux skills, not “sudo apt-get.”
- **Distributed systems**: consensus (Raft, Paxos), scaling, data replication, message queues (Kafka).
- **Networking protocols**: TCP/IP, HTTP/2, QUIC, gRPC. (Use your telecom roots here as a weapon.)
- **Databases**: internals of Postgres, Redis, Cassandra. Learn how they work under the hood.
- **Cloud infrastructure**: AWS first, then GCP/Azure. Learn Terraform, Kubernetes, CI/CD.

This isn’t trivia. This is the difference between “mid-level dev” and “we’ll pay you $180k to keep our systems alive.”

- **Security**: AppSec, cloud security. Companies love a paranoid engineer.
